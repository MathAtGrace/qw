Mathematical Formulas.

Simple Interest
\begin{equation*} I=Prt \text{ or } A=P+Prt \end{equation*}
Compound Interest
\begin{equation*} A=P\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt} \text{ or } P=\frac{A}{\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}} \end{equation*}
Continuously Compounded
\begin{equation*} A=Pe^{rt} \text{ or }P=\frac{A}{e^{rt}} \end{equation*}
Savings Plans
\begin{equation*} A=\frac{d\left(\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}-1\right)}{\left(\frac{r}{n}\right)} \text{ or } d=\frac{A\left(\frac{r}{n}\right)}{\left(\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}-1\right)} \end{equation*}
Loans
\begin{equation*} P=\frac{d\left(1-\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{-nt}\right)}{\frac{r}{n}} \text{ or } d=\frac{P\left(\frac{r}{n}\right)}{\left(1-\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{-nt}\right)} \end{equation*}
\(P\)
is the principal, starting amount, or present value
\(d\)
is your loan payment (your monthly payment, annual payment, etc.)
\(r\)
is the annual intrest rate in decimal form
\(n\)
is the number of compounding periods in one year
\(t\)
is the length of the loan, in years
\(A\)
is the end amunt or future value
If the compounding frequency is not always explicitly given, it is determined by how often you make payments
