There is evidence that smiling can attenuate judgments of possible wrongdoing. This phenomenon termed the "smile-leniency effect" was the focus of a study by Marianne LaFrance & Marvin Hecht in 1995 5 . The following data are measurements of how lenient the sentences were for three different types of smiles and one neutral control. A higher number indicates greater leniency. The same subject was used for all of the conditions so that may affect the results.

  1. Make a histogram for each smile type and the neutral control.

  2. Find the mean for each type of smile and the neutral control.

  3. Find the median for each type of smile and the neutral control.

  4. Compare the shapes of the distributions as well as the centers for each type of smile and control.
