Activity 3.3.4. Sal, Cal, and Val.

Sal, Cal and Val are triplets.
  • Sal gets a paper route at age 14 and decides to invest $150 each month in a mutual fund which averages a 10% annual return. Sal only makes deposits for 5 years and then leaves the balance to compound until retirement at age 65.
  • Cal is impressed by Sal’s bank account, and gets a job in college at age 19 and dutifully makes deposits of $150 each month into the same mutual fund for 9 years and then leaves the balance to compound until retirement at age 65.
  • Val partied a lot growing up, but then gets a good job at the age of 27 and diligently makes deposits of $150 each month all the way to retirement at the age of 65.
Answer the following questions:
  1. How much money did each brother invest?
  2. How much does each brother have saved for retirement at age 65?
  3. Put the interest rate of 10% into a cell in your spreadsheet, then redo your formulas for Item b so that they reference this cell. Try changing the interest rate until Sal and Cal end up with the same amount at age 65. What is this interest rate?
  4. What interest rate causes Cal and Val to end up with the same amount at age 65?
