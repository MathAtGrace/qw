Solution 4.1.16.1.

If the margin of error was calculated to be plus or minus $85.63, then with a confidence level of 95% we could say that the average amount spent by the population is somewhere between $365.69 and $536.95.
These values are found by computing \(\$451.32 - \$85.63 = \$365.69\) and \(\$451.32 + \$85.63 = \$536.95\)
We could also write this as a confidence interval: ($365.69, $536.95).
Now we have come full circle and seen how we can use data from a sample to estimate the parameter we were interested in for our population.
