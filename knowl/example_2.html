Example 6.2.2.

In our election from above, we had this preference table:

Number of voters 1 3 3 3
1st choice A A O H
2nd choice O H H A
3rd choice H O A O

For the plurality method, we only look at the 1st choice row. Totaling them up:

  • Ana: \(1+3 = 4\) votes

  • Omar: \(3\) votes

  • Helena: \(3\) votes

Ana is the winner using the plurality voting method.

Notice that Ana won with 4 out of 10 votes, or 40% of the votes, which is a plurality of the votes, but not a majority.
