Activity 2.2.4. Estimate, then Calculate.

For each situation below, describe your estimation strategy for each situation. Then estimate without a calculator. Then and only then go back use a calculator to refine your estimate.
  1. About 3 million working people in the U.S. make at or below the federal minimum wage of $7.25 per hour. Estimate the amount of money needed per year to raise their wages to $15 per hour.
  2. The amount of money needed to buy everyone in your state a box of Girl Scout cookies.
  3. The amount of money needed to send all graduating high school seniors in the United States to college for four years.
  4. If the U.S. national debt was $22 trillion in 2019, and was evenly divided among the people living in the United States, how much would each person’s share be?
  5. If the U.S. budget is about $4.1 trillion, estimate the percentage of the budget which would be needed to pay for your estimate in Item c above.
