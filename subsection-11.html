    Subsection A.2 Cell References

    One of the powerful things about spreadsheets is using a cell reference, such as C5 in a calculation. When you use a cell reference, the values will automatically update if any of the referenced values change.

    Let‚Äôs make a spreadsheet for the percentage tip example above. We calculated an 18% tip on a bill of $35.75. We might want to tip 18% in general, but our bill will change values. We labeled the first column Bill Amount and the second column Tip. The amount of $35.75 is entered in cell A2. Then when we write our formula in B2, we want to calculate 18% of A2. That way if the number in A2 changes, our tip will automatically update.

    The formula

    =0.18*A2

    is entered in B2 which gives a result of $6.44 when you hit enter.

    When the bill amount is changed, the tip is recalculated.
